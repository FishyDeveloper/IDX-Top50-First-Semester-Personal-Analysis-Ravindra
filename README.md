## Analysis

### Macro Sector Dynamics
*Dynamic market share distribution tracking sector dominance shifting month-by-month.*
![Macro Sector Concentration](Visuals/sector_concentration.png)

The financial sector maintains the highest relative concentration within IHSG, exhibiting a strong upward trend throughout the semester. Initially, the financial sector has an approximately 24.3% of the top 50 market share in January, the sector steadily expanded its structural footprint to close May as the undisputed index heavyweight at roughly 30.4%. The infrastructure sector holds the position of second-highest concentration within the cohort. After anchoring around 19.96% in January, its weight distribution exhibited strong resilience, oscillating between the 16.88% and 20.3% thresholds across the remainder of the semester.

A similar structural resilience is exhibited by the Technology sector. Despite maintaining a modest baseline footprint with 6.5% on average of the top 50 index weight, the sector consistently defended its market share without experiencing the severe compression seen in other sectors. Consumer non-cyclicals (4.76% average) and Industrials (5.14% average) owns a similar trajectory of structural resilience. Despite their lower concentration profiles, their relative weights remained stable throughout the five-month period.

The lower-weight spectrum reveals a trajectory of steady structural accretion in both Healthcare and Real Estate. Healthcare successfully scaled its index footprint from 1.82% to 2.36% over the five-month timeline. Mirroring this upward movement, Properties & Real Estate systematically elevated its position from 2.57% in January to 3.09% in May.

Conversely, the Energy and Basic Materials sectors experienced a pronounced structural contraction within the top 50 hierarchy across the first semester. Basic Materials compressed from a 17.34% concentration down to 13.17% over the five-month period. The downward trajectory is more visible in the Energy sector, which underwent a sharp relative weight erosion, dropping from 16.68% in January to close May at 11.80%. The most dramatic contraction occurs within the Consumer Cyclicals sector. Initially capturing a measly 1.46% of the top 50 concentration weight in January, its presence compressed to 0.87% in February before completely exiting the top 50 cohort boundaries for the remainder of the semester (March through May).
